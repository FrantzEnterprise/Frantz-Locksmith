Surgical Drilling — A Liberty Safe Repair Walkthrough

By Robert Frantz • May 2026 • CA LCO 4160

When I have to drill into a safe, I do it like a surgeon — one clean hole, exactly where it needs to be, and then I restore it so the safe is as good as new. Here's a recent Liberty Plate Safe repair, start to finish.

🎯 One clean 1/4" hole — exactly where it needs to be

🔧 Tapered steel pin seated — integrity restored

🛡️ Offset plate — different drill point if someone targets the pin

✅ Pin leveled inside — lock body mounts flush

✅ Pin leveled outside — bezel and dial align perfectly

✨ New S&G lock, bezel, Liberty dial — custom combo set

🏁 Adjusted, inspected, lubricated — functions like new

One clean hole. One tapered pin. One offset plate. One safe restored to factory condition.
That's the difference 40+ years of experience makes.

What Makes This Different From a Hack Job?

An amateur drills wherever they guess the lock is. They punch through hardplate, miss the relocker, and leave you with a safe that's compromised — fireproofing destroyed, bolts damaged, and sometimes impossible to repair.

I drill one small hole in exactly the right spot. I know where the lock is, where the relocker is, and where the hardplate ends. Then I restore the safe so it's as strong as it was the day it left the factory — and in some cases, stronger.

The offset mounting plate is a good example. I drill the new lock's mounting holes in a different pattern from where the old ones were. If someone ever tries to drill out the repair pin, they'll hit the wrong spot. That's not just a repair — that's future-proofing.

Level on Both Sides

Inside and out. The pin gets ground flush on the inside so the new lock body sits perfectly flat. Then ground flush on the outside so the dial bezel mounts true and the dial spins smooth. No binding, no stress, no premature wear.

It takes an extra 10 minutes. It's the difference between a repair that looks like it was done by a pro and one that looks like it was done by a guy with a drill and a prayer.

The Result

New Sargent & Greenleaf lock. New bezel. New Liberty dial. Custom combination set, tested five times with the door open before closing it. The safe functions like brand new.

This is what you're paying for when you hire a real safe technician. Not a patch job. Not a "good enough." A restoration that preserves the value, security, and fire rating of your safe.
