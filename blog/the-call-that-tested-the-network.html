The Call That Tested the Network

May 2026 • By Frantz Locksmith Service • Network Probate Legacy

The call came in earlier in the day. A client in a bind — and I mean a real bind.

Here's the situation: a family had purchased a safe from a fellow locksmith friend of mine about 15 years ago. Solid guy, known him for years, does good work. But he'd been injured and couldn't help open it now. The family needed that safe opened — not just opened, but opened on a strict timeline.

It was a probate situation. Multiple family members had to coordinate schedules to all be present at the same time. Add law enforcement to that mix — they needed to be there to witness and verify the contents, because there was a will believed to be inside. Everyone had carved out their Friday afternoon. The meeting was set for 4:00 PM sharp.

When they called me, I was already out of town on a job. I looked at the clock, ran the math, and knew — even if I dropped everything right then, I couldn't guarantee I'd make it on time. And honestly, I probably would have been late. These people had already been through enough. They didn't need a safe tech showing up after everyone else had arrived, keeping a room full of people waiting including law enforcement.

So I did the only thing that made sense. I called my protégé.

Enter Mr. Glazer

Mr. Glazer — the man I trained, the one who learned the old school way — had just opened his own company: Glazer Safe & Lock. He was out there building his own reputation, and I knew exactly what he was capable of. I've said it before and I'll say it again: I trained him right. No shortcuts. No torches unless there's no other way. Precision, patience, integrity.

I called him, explained the situation, and asked if he could make it happen. He didn't hesitate.

Mr. Glazer arrived on time. Not fashionably late, not "I'll be there in 20" late — on time. The family was there. Law enforcement was there. The safe was opened expediently — non-destructively, the way it should be done. The contents were verified. The will was found. And Mr. Glazer repaired the safe afterward so it could continue serving the family, just as it had for the last 15 years.

That Friday at 4 PM went from a panic to a relief, all because of one phone call and a network built on trust.

"The person who called us was in panic mode after finding out my locksmith friend was injured and had to cancel. But he knew what to do. He gave us the number, and all went well thanks to our high security network of trusted old school experts with integrity."

How We Roll

This is what I mean when I say we're old school. The old school way isn't just about the tools you use or the techniques you favor. It's about who you know and who you can count on. It's about training the next generation so well that when you can't be there, he can — and he'll do it just as good, if not better.

Mr. Glazer isn't just my protégé. He's proof that the craft is alive. That there are still young guys coming up who care about doing it right. And when you've got a network of people like that — people with integrity, skill, and the willingness to drop what they're doing to help — there's almost nothing we can't handle.

So call anytime. Even if I can't be there myself, I know someone who can. We'll make the impossible happen.
